HYMN III. Aśvins

YE Aśvins, rich in treasure, Lords of splendour, having nimble hands, accept the sacred food.

Ye Aśvins, rich in wondrous deeds, ye heroes worthy of our praise, accept our songs with mighty thought.

Nāsatyas, wonder-workers, yours are these libations with clipt grass: Come ye whose paths are red with flame.

O Indra marvellously bright, come, these libations long for thee, thus by fine fingers purified.

Urged by the holy singer, sped by song, come, Indra, Of the libation-pouring priest.

Approach, O Indra, hasting thee, Lord of Bay Horses.

In our libation take delight.

Ye Devas, who protect, reward, and cherish men, approach your worshipper's drink-offering.

Ye Devas, swift at work, come hither quickly to the draught, as milch-kine hasten to their stalls.

The Devas, accept the sacred draught.

Wealthy in spoil, enriched with hymns, with eager love, our offering.
