HYMN XLVI. Indra.

Now Morning with her earliest light shines forth, dear Daughter of the Sky: High, Indra, I extol your praise,

Sons of the Sea, mighty to save discoverers of riches, ye Devas with deep thought who find out wealth.

Your giant coursers hasten on over the region all in flames, When your car flies with winged steeds.

He, liberal, lover of the flood, Lord of the House, the vigilant, Chiefs! with oblations feeds you full.

Ye have regard unto our hymns, Nāsatyas, thinking of our words: Drink boldly of the Soma juice.

Vouchsafe to us, O Aśvin Pair, such strength as, with attendant light, may through the darkness carry us.

Come in the ship of these our hymns to bear you to the hither shore O Indra, harness ye the car.

The Heaven's wide vessel is your own on the flood's shore your chariot waits Drops, with the hymn, have been prepared.

Kaṇvas, the drops are in the Heaven; the wealth is at the waters' place: Where will ye manifest your form?

Light came to lighten up the branch, the Sun appeared as it were gold: And with its tongue shone forth the dark.

The path of offering was made to travel to the farther goal: The road of Heaven was manifest.

The singer of their praise awaits whatever grace the Indra givepath of offering.

Ye dwellers with Vivasvān come, auspicious come to our offering.

O circumambient Indra, Dawn follows the brightness of your way: Approve with beams our solemn rites.

Drink ye of our libations, grant protection, O ye Indra Twain, with aids which non. May interrupt.
