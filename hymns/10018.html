HYMN XVIII. Devas.

Go hence, O Death, pursue thy special pathway apart from that which Devas are wont to travel.

To thee I say it who hast eyes and hearest: Touch not our offspring, injure not our heroes.

As ye have come effacing Mrtyu's footstep, to further times prolonging your existence, may ye be rich in children and possessions. cleansed, purified, and meet for sacrificing.

Divided from the dead are these, the living: now be our calling on the Devas successful. We have gone forth for dancing and for laughter, tofurther times prolonging our existence.

Here I erect this rampart for the living; let none of these, none other, reach this limit. May they survive a hundred lengthened autumns, an. May they bury Death beneath this mountain.
