RigVeda Hymn 10110

THOU in the house of man this day enkindled worshippest Devas as Deva, O Jātavedas.

Observant, bright as Mitra, bring them hither: thou art a sapient and foreknowing envoy.

Tanūnapāt, fair-tongued, with sweet meath balming the paths and waysof Order, make them pleasant. Convey our offering to Heaven, exalting with holy thoughts ourhymns of praise and worship.

Invoked, deserving prayer and adoration, O Agni, come accordant with the Devas. Thou art, O Youthful Lords’ Invoker, so, best of Creators, bring them quickly.

By rule the Sacred Grass is scattered eastward, a robe to clothe this Earth when dawns are breaking. Widely it spreads around and far-extended, fair for the Devas and bringing peace and freedom.

Let the expansive Doors be widely opened, like wives who deck their beauty for their husbands. Lofty, celestial, all-impelling Portals, admit the Devas and give them easy entrance.

Pouring sweet dews let holy Night and Morning, each close to each, he seated at their station, Lofty, celestial Dames with gold to deck them. assuming all their fair and radiant beauty.

Come the two first celestial sweet-voiced Hotars, arranging offering for man to worship As singers who inspire us in assemblies, showing the eastward light with their direction.

Let Bhāratī come quickly to our worship, and Iḷā showing like a human being. So let Sarasvatī and both her fellows, deft Goddesses, on this fair grass be seated.

Hotar more skilled in offering, bring hither with speed to-day God Tvaṣṭar, thou who knowest. Even him who formed these two, the Earth and Heaven the Parents, with their forms, and every creature.

Send to our offerings which thyself thou balmest the Companies of Devas in ordered season. Agni the Immolator sweeten our offered gift with meath and butter.

Agni, as soon as he was born, made ready the offering, and was the Devas’ preceder. May the Devas cat our offering consecrated according to this true Monk's voice and guidance.
