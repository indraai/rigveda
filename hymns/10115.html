HYMN CXV. Agni.

VERILY wondrous is the tender Youngling's growth who never draweth nigh to drink his Mothers' milk.

As soon as she who hath no udder bore him, he, faring on his great errand, suddenly grew strong.

Then Agni was his name, most active to bestow, gathering up the trees with his consuming tooth; Skilled in fair offering, armed with destroying tongue, impetuous as a bull that snorteth in the mead.

Praise him, yourDeva who, bird-like, rests upon a tree, scattering drops of juice and pouring forth his flood, Speaking aloud with flame as with his lips a priest, and broadening his paths like one of high command.

Thou Everlasting, whom, far-striding fain to burn, the winds, uninterrupted, never overcome, they have approached, as warriors eager for the fight, heroic Trita, guiding him to gain his wish.
