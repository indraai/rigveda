HYMN XLIX. Agni.

AGNI, come hither with thy fires; we choose thee as Invoking Monk.

Let the extended ladle full of oil balm thee, best Monk, to sit on sacred grass.

Agni, thou art Disposer, Sage. Herald, bright Deva! and worshipful, best offerer, cheerful, to be praised in holy rites, pure Lord! by singers with their hymns.

Most Youthful and Eternal, bring the longing Devas to me, the guileless, for the feast. Come, Vasu, to the banquet that is well-prepared: rejoice thee, gracious, with our songs.

Famed art thou, Agni, far and wide, Preserver, righteous, and a Sage. The holy singers, O refulgent kindled Deva! arrangers, call on thee to come.

Shine, most Resplendent! blaze, send bliss unto the folk, and to thy worshipper Great art thou. May my princes, with good fires, subduing foes, rest in the keeping of the Devas.

O Agni, as thou burnest down to Earth even high-grown underwood, So, bright as Mitra is, burn him who injures us, him who plots ill against thy friend.

Give us not as a prey to mortal enemy, nor to the wicked friend of fiends. With conquering guards, auspicious, unassailable, protect us, O Most Youthful Deva.

Protect us, Agni, through the first, protect us through the second hymn, Protect us through three hymns, O Lord of Power and Might, through four hymns, Vasu, guard thou us.

Preserve us from each fiend who brings the Devas no gift, preserve thou us in deeds of strength: For we possess in thee the nearest Friend of all, for service of the Devas and weal.

O Holy Agni, give us wealth renowned with men and strengthening life. Bestow on us, O Helper, that which many crave, more glorious still by righteousness;
